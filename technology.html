Our Technology

SPIO — Stacked Planar Integrated Optics — is a production technology that allows mass production of small-size optical devices with photonic component integration.

How It Works

The SPIO Process

Four integrated steps — from optical design through production, precision assembly, and finishing — deliver compact, fully passive optical modules at scale.

1

Optical Design

We convert your free-space optical setups into designs that can be realised as a compact SPIO module using Zemax simulations and close customer collaboration.

2

Production

We print your optics in polymer on glass wafers using nanoimprint lithography. Hundreds of module parts are produced on single wafers at high throughput.

3

Assembly

We align and stack the wafers to micrometer precision, bonding them with optical-grade adhesive and spacing them using precision spacers such as spheres.

4

Finishing

The stacked wafers are singulated into separate SPIO modules — fully functional, passive optical components measuring just a few millimetres.

Why SPIO

The Advantages of Our Approach

Stacked Planar Integrated Optics offers a unique combination of performance, miniaturisation, and manufacturing scalability.

📈

Scalable Manufacturing

Our roll-to-plate nanoimprint approach prints hundreds of module parts per wafer, enabling cost-effective scaling from prototype to mass production without retooling.

📏

Miniaturisation

Finished SPIO modules measure only a few millimetres — ideal for space-constrained applications in consumer electronics, medical devices, and industrial sensors.

🔭

Photonic Integration

The SPIO process natively supports integration of photonic components — lenses, mirrors, gratings, optical coatings, and freeform optics — within a single wafer-based module.

🎯

Micrometer Precision

Wafer-level alignment and bonding delivers micrometer-class precision in every module, ensuring consistent optical performance across high-volume production runs.

💰

Low Cost per Module

Polymer imprint technology on standard glass wafers dramatically reduces material and process costs compared to traditional precision optics manufacturing.

⚙

Passive Components

SPIO modules are fully passive optical components — no active alignment required in assembly — reducing integration complexity and total system cost for our customers.
